# The Shed — Visual Identity Ideas

Parked from 2026-04-08 design session. These are the ideas that would make the app feel like *The Shed* and not just another dashboard.

## What makes it feel generic right now

The app uses standard component library patterns (Shadcn cards, form inputs, data tables) with a custom color palette. Swapping colors doesn't change the fundamental feel. What's missing is visual elements that could ONLY belong to a music practice app.

## Ideas to explore

### Waveform / rhythm visualization
- Subtle waveform in the background of the practice timer that responds to audio input or BPM
- Could be a thin line across the top of the page, or a full ambient background
- When the metronome is running, the waveform pulses in time

### Instrument-specific visual identity
- Each instrument page has its own subtle character — different accent color, different ambient visual
- Guitar page might feel warm/analog, drums page might feel percussive/sharp, keys might feel smooth/flowing
- Not just a color swap — different micro-interactions or background textures per instrument

### Practice timer that feels physical
- The timer itself could look like an analog clock face or a vinyl record spinning
- BPM display could pulse like a heartbeat, not just show a number
- The "session active" state could feel like being inside a recording studio — focused, warm, immersive

### Session history as a timeline
- Instead of a data table, show practice sessions as a visual timeline or musical staff
- Each session is a "note" on the staff — longer sessions are longer notes
- Streaks visible as connected phrases

### Practice journal feel
- The whole app should feel like opening your practice notebook
- Handwritten-style elements mixed with clean UI (not literally handwriting fonts, but that warmth)
- Personal, warm, like it knows you — "welcome back" that references your last session specifically

### Ambient audio cues
- Subtle click or tone when starting a session
- A gentle "done" sound when stopping
- The app SOUNDS like a music tool, not just looks like one

## Priority

Pick ONE of these to prototype first. The waveform visualization or the instrument-specific identity would have the biggest impact on first impression.
